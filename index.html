About LeBron

LeBron James was born on December 30, 1984, in Akron, Ohio. The highest scoring player of all time, LeBron has spent over two decades dominating the NBA. Through his incredible athleticism and versatility, he has redefined what it means to be a complete player.

LeBron became the NBA's all-time leading scorer in 2023, surpassing Kareem Abdul-Jabbar's 40 year long record. He is a 4x NBA Champion, 4x Finals MVP, and 4x regular season MVP.

Career Timeline

LeBron has played for three franchises across his legendary career:

  1. Cleveland Cavaliers (2003–2010)
    • Drafted 1st overall in 2003
    • Named Rookie of the Year
    • Led Cavs to their first NBA Finals in 2007
    • Multiple MVP awards
  2. Miami Heat (2010–2014)
    • Formed the "Big Three" with Wade and Bosh
    • Won back-to-back championships in 2012 and 2013
    • 2x Finals MVP
  3. Cleveland Cavaliers (2014–2018)
    • Returned home to Cleveland
    • Won the 2016 NBA Championship
    • Delivered Cleveland its first major sports title in 52 years
  4. Los Angeles Lakers (2018–Present)
    • Won the 2020 NBA Championship in the Bubble
    • Became the NBA's all-time leading scorer in 2023
    • Still playing at an elite level
